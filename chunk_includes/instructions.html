In this experiment, you will be doing two tasks at the same time. First, at the start of each trial, you will see grids like this one:

After a short period of time, these grids disappear. At the end of each trial, you are presented with an empty grid and you must recreate the pattern that you saw at the start of the trial. It is important that you recreate the grid as accurately as possible.

In between, you will read a sentence and then your task is to decide whether the pictured object followed has been mentioned in the preceding sentence. Press ‘1’ if the pictured object has been mentioned in the preceding sentence; otherwise press ‘2’.

Both your reaction times and accuracy will be measured, so it is important for you to make the decisions about the picture as quickly and accurately as possible. Please keep your hand over ‘1’ and ‘2’ for the entirety of the experiment, so that you won't be slowed down by unnecessary movements.

Sometimes after recreating the pattern, you will receive a question about the sentence you just read. Please respond to the question as accurately as possible by pressing ‘1’ or ‘2’. There is no time pressure on responding such question.
